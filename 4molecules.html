Biological Molecules

Author: Ms. Joey Lie (EI)

Carbohydrate

  • Function: Source of Energy
  • Composition: Carbon, Hydrogen, Oxygen
  • Monosaccharide
    • One-ring sugar, example:
    • Glucose (used for respiration)
    • Galactose
    • Fructose
  • Disaccharide
    • Two-ring sugar, example:
    • Maltose
      • Glucose + Glucose
    • Lactose
      • Glucose + Galactose
    • Sucrose
      • Glucose + Fructose
  • Polysaccharide
    • Three-ring sugar, example:
    • Starch
      • Storage of carbohydrate in plant cell
    • Glycogen
      • Storage of carbohydrate in animal cell (usually stored in liver)
    • Cellulose
      • Componenet of plant cell wall

Protein

  • Is used for: Growth, Producing Cells, Ezymes, Hormones, Antibodies
  • Is a chain of amino acids
  • Composition: Carbon, Hydroge, Oxygem, Nitrogen (sometimes Sulfur and Phosphorus)
  • Test of Protein (Biuret Solution):
    • Positive = purple
    • Negative = blue

Fat

  • Function: Insulation, Storage
  • Composition: Carbon, Hydrogen, Oxygen
  • Fats can be broken into fatty acids & glycerol through hydrolysis/enzymes
  • Test of Fat (Ethanol Test)
    • Positive = Cloudy, emulsion is formed
    • Negative = Clear
  • Fun fact, the fat molecules in your body doesn't reduce in number, it only reduces in size


DNA

  • Double Helix strand
  • Base: Adenine(A), Guanine(G), Thymine(T), Cytosine(C)
  • Match: A-T, G-C
