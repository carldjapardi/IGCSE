Respiration

Respiration is the oxidative breakdown of organic substances with the release of energy.

Our cells need energy for:

  • Contracting muscle
  • Making proteins molecules from amino acids
  • Cell division
  • Active transport
  • Transmitting nerve impulses
  • Porduce heat inside the body

The main nutrient to provide energy in cless is glucose

  • Aerobic respiration - the chemical reactions in cells that use oxygen to break down nutrient molecules to release energy. Most of the steps in aerobic respiration take place inside mitochondria.
    glucose + oxygen --> carbon dioxide + water
    C6H12O6 --> 6CO2 + 6H2O
  • Anaerobic respiration - the chemical reactions in cells that break down nutrient molecules to release energy without using oxygen.

    Anaerobic respiration of yeast (during alcoholic fermentation):
    glucose --> alcohol + carbon dioxide
    C6H12O6 --> C2H5OH + 2CO2


    Muscle cells can undergo anaerobic respiration for short duration when insufficient oxygen is available for anaerobic respiration (e.g during vigorous exercise):
    glucose --> lactic acid
    C6H12O6 --> 2C3H6O3
