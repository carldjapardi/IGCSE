Organisation Of The Organism

Author: Ms. Joey Lie (EI)

Building Blocks

  • Cells - cells is the smallest unit of life. It is usually made of a nucleus, cytoplasm, and tiny vacuoles.
  • Tissues - a group of cells working together will form a tissue (i.e muscle tissue, mesophyll)
  • Organs - a group of tissues working together will form an organ (i.e heart, leaf)
  • Organ systems - a group of organs working together will form an organ system (i.e respiratory system)
  • Human Body - a group of organ systems working together will form a human

Cells

  • An animal cell is generally made up of different organelles that includes:
    • Nuclues - Description: nuch more dense than cytoplasm, has a nuclear envelope. Function: Controls all cell activities, plays a key role in cell division, contain genetic information.
      • The inside of a nucleus can only be seen through an electron microscope.
      • The nucleus is made up of the nuclear envelope, the nucleoplasm and one or more nucleoli (nucleolus).
      • Long thread – like structures in the nucleus, called chromatin, are made of proteins and deoxyribonucleic acid (DNA). DNA stores hereditary information and instructions for cellular activities and cell division.
      • Chromatin threads are critical to cell division. During this process, they condense into highly coiled structures called chromosomes.
      • Nucleoli are responsible for the manufacture of proteins.
    • Cytoplasm
      • The cytoplasm is the site for chemical reactions. Here other organelles such as:
        • Rough Endoplasmic Reticulum (RER) - Description: A network of membranes, with small round structures (called ribosomes attached). Function: The ribosomes synthesise proteins for transport out of the cell, which the RER processes
        • Smooth Endoplasmic Reticulum (SER) - Description: A network of membranes without ribosomes. Function: Systhesises fats and steriouds, helps to detoxify the cell by converting harmful substances into harmless materials
        • Golgi Appartus - Description: A stack of flattened discs which comprise thin spaces surrounded by membranes. Function: Modifies and repackages substances from the ER into vesicles for export out the cell
        • Mitochondria - Description: shaped like a sausage with intricate internal folds. Function: site of aerobic respiration
        • Centriole - Description: Hollow, smal cylinders. Function: helps in cell division
        • vesicle
    • Cell membrane - Controls the movement of substances into and out of the cell; selectively permeable
    • Tiny vacuoles - Description: temporary, tiny, more than one. Function: vacuoles are generally small and help sequester waste products
  • A plant cell is typically made up of different organelles that includes:
    • Nuclues - similar to that of animal cell
    • Cell membrane - similar to that of animal cell
    • Cell Wall - Description: The outermost part of the cell. Composed of cellulose. Freely permeable to small molecules. Function: Protects the plant cell from injury. Gives the plant cell a fixed shape
    • Vacuole - Description: Large, contains sap. Function: Provides shape for the plant, mantain water balance.
    • Cytoplasm - The cytoplasm in plant cell is similar to that of the animal cell except that centrioles is not found in plant cell, the vacuole in plant cell is huge and only one, and chloroplast is present in plant cells
      • Chloroplast - Description: Oval shaped, contains chlorophyll which is green. Function: Used to trap light in photosynthesis

Specialised Cells

  • Red Blood Cell (RBC) - Found in blood. It's primary purpose is to transport oxygen. It contains haemoglobin which will bind to oxygen. A RBC doesn't have a nucleues in order to tranport more oxygen. It also has a biconcave shape to increase surface area.
  • Muscle Cell - they have many protein fibres in them allowing it to be contracted
  • Ciliated Cell - has hairs (cilia) which is used to sweep mucus upwards. It is also used to trapped dust and bacteria
  • Nerve Cell - it conducts nerve impulses. They have an axon that is convered with insulation to prevent the electrical impulse from weakening.


  • Root Hair Cell - absorbs minerals and water from the soil. It has a long extnesion to increase the surface area to absorb better
  • Xylem Vessel - they have no end to end wall so that substances can be tranported with ease. The are also contain lignin to make it waterproof and strong.

How Do We See Cells?

We do this by using a light microscop or electron microscope. A light microscope is able to produce colored image with a resolution of 200nm. While a electron microscope is able to produce a grayscale image, but a resolution of 0.2nm.

Exam tip: usually, igcse exam papers will ask you to determine the actual size of an object. In this case use the formula:
magnification = image / actual
